6.1 Purpose of the Initial Call
The initial call is NOT a technical design meeting.
Its purpose is to: understand the client’s problem (not just their request), clarify scope/stage/constraints, establish procurement route and team structure, and set expectations about CLW’s role and limitations.
6.2 Initial Call Checklist
  • Project overview: name, location and statusCall
  • Basement / waterproofing risks known to dateCall
  • Client objectives and sensitivities (programme, cost, risk)Call
  • Design responsibility and CDP interfacesCall
  • Required outputs and timescalesCall
  • Appointment route and decision-maker identifiedCall
  • Client name, organisation and source of enquiryCall
  • Post-call internal note written (key risks, likely scope, exclusions, fee concerns)Required
Responsibility: Person receiving the enquiry → Directors
6.3 Initial Call — Sales Framework
Initial Call (3-min vet)▾
Are they a good fit? · Build rapport · Where are they now? · Where do they want to get to? · What’s their why? · Sprinkle credibility · Agree next steps
Project Familiarisation (before Discovery Call)▾
Google Maps 3D review · Planning portal check · ROI content preparation · ROI calculation

Proposal Graphics: Section through development (identify earth-retaining/waterproofing perimeter) · General arrangement plans · Identify complex areas

Site Investigation: Identify highest likely groundwater level

Flood Risk Assessment: Assess flood risk from FRA
Discovery Call▾
Confirm fit · Rapport · Where are they now (Risk Report) · Where do they want to get to · What’s their why · Sprinkle credibility · Next steps: Goldilocks / Enhanced / Basic? · Information request
Goldilocks Presentation▾
Rapport · Where are they now · Where do they want to get to · What’s their why · Sprinkle credibility · Present three options (Basic / Recommended / Enhanced) · Agree next steps
6.4 Suitability Triage
Before committing time to a fee proposal, CLW undertakes a quick internal triage.
  • Project within CLW’s technical competence?Gate
  • Scale & risk proportionate to CLW’s PI cover? (refer to CLW - Finance/_Insurances/2025-2026)Gate
  • Clear role for CLW (not duplicating others)?Gate
  • No conflicts of interest?Gate
  • No red flags (programme pressure, fee pressure, unclear responsibilities)?Gate
If unsuitable, CLW will politely decline and may suggest alternatives.

8.1 Purchase Order / Written Instruction
No services shall formally commence until a valid PO or written instruction is received. Where a PO is not available, written confirmation to proceed must be received and recorded. Any “at risk” instruction must be approved internally and clearly documented.
CLW will NOT rely on verbal instructions alone · Will NOT accept retrospective POs without written confirmation of scope and fee
Once PO received, store in the appropriate folder within the project folder. Responsibility: Directors / Senior Lead
Appointment Checklist
  • PO or written instruction received & filed in project folderGate
  • Contract / Terms of Appointment issued & agreed (CLW standard terms)Gate
  • Project set up in Hub — name, client, fee value, stage breakdown, project leadRequired
  • Project email address created and CC’d for formal correspondence & audit trailDirectors
  • HubSpot updated: client contact details, project status, source of instruction, key milestonesDirectors
  • Resourcing confirmed: internal resource, anticipated time commitments, key deliverable datesLead
  • Any third-party terms reviewed before acceptance — deviations approved internallyRequired
8.5 Resourcing
Resource allocation shall be reviewed against: current workload · programme risk · fee constraints.
No staff member shall be over-allocated beyond reasonable capacity. Changes to scope or programme must trigger a resourcing review.
Responsibility: Directors / Senior Lead

Stage 0 — Familiarisation
Establish informed baseline understanding of site, existing conditions, and any historic/current waterproofing strategy
9.1.1 Brief Site Visit — Pre-Attendance Checks
  • Confirm meeting location, access arrangements and site induction requirements
  • Confirm required PPE
  • Confirm no asbestos removal or disturbance works are ongoing
  • Confirm meeting agenda has been issued
  • Confirm list of attendees and their roles
On site: visual non-intrusive inspection only · Identify waterproofing measures, defects, constraints · Take representative photographs where permitted. No intrusive works. No supervision of site activities.
9.1.2 Desktop Study
  • Architectural drawings and specifications
  • Structural drawings and calculations (where available)
  • Site investigation and geotechnical reports
  • Existing waterproofing drawings, details, or reports
  • Historic maintenance or defect information
9.1.3 RFI & 9.1.4 Initial Assessment
  • Issue formal RFI where information is missing, unclear or contradictory
  • Record assumptions clearly if information cannot be obtained
  • Attempt to identify as-built waterproofing design
  • High-level assessment of apparent waterproofing performance
  • High-level commentary on observed risks and limitations
  • Contribute to defining scope of any proposed intrusive investigations
  • Review any existing or proposed waterproofing strategy
  • Prepare high-level options and appraisal where requested
Typical Output: High-Level Waterproofing Report (advisory)
Stage 2 — Concept Design
Establish appropriate waterproofing strategy aligned with project risk, use and performance expectations
9.2.1 Design Information Review
  • Review architectural concept proposals
  • Review structural design principles
  • Review geotechnical and groundwater information
9.2.2 Performance Definition
  • Identify required waterproofing performance grades per BS 8102
  • Consider use, occupancy and tolerance to moisture ingress
9.2.3 Risk Assessment
  • Groundwater levels and variability
  • Soil conditions and hydrostatic risk
  • Structural form and buildability
  • Consequence of failure
9.2.4 Strategy Development
  • Advise on appropriate waterproofing approach (Type A, B, C, or combined)
  • Identify key interfaces with structure and external envelope
  • Highlight areas of elevated risk requiring further development
Outputs: Stage 2 Waterproofing Design Report · Meeting attendance · Pre-purchase/pre-planning feasibility input · Input to outline specifications and cost plan assumptions
Stage 3 — Spatial Coordination
Spatially coordinate the waterproofing strategy and reduce construction and interface risk
9.3.1 Detailed Risk Assessment
  • Drainage strategy review
  • Hydrostatic pressures assessment
  • Construction tolerances and defects allowance
9.3.2 Design Review & 9.3.3 Coordination
  • Review and comment on architectural and structural drawings
  • Identify clashes, omissions and continuity risks
  • Coordinate with the wider design team
  • Contribute to coordinated draft performance specification
  • Review early value-engineering proposals for risk impact
  • Provide advisory comments on sequencing and allowances
Drawings Included in Stage 3 Pack
  • Site conceptual model
  • Required grades of waterproofing performance
  • Indicative waterproofing strategy
Design Philosophy Report — Contents
  • Site conceptual model
  • Waterproofing risk assessment
  • Required grades of waterproofing performance
  • Outline waterproofing strategy
  • Draft performance specification
Output: Stage 3 Waterproofing Design Philosophy Pack
Stage 4a — Outline Design
Translate the agreed strategy into coordinated outline design information
9.4.1 Design Development
  • Develop the coordinated Stage 3 waterproofing information
  • Prepare waterproofing layout and key detail sheets
  • Develop movement joint principles where required
9.4.2 Specification & 9.4.3 Engagement
  • Contribute to the coordinated waterproofing performance specification
  • Review early contractor value-engineering proposals
  • Attend and contribute to technical workshop with the contractor (where appointed)
Stage 4b — Technical Design
Verify compliance of specialist proposals with the agreed waterproofing strategy
9.5.1 Contractor Proposal Review
  • Review specialist waterproofing contractor / subcontractor proposals
  • Respond to technical queries relating to BS 8102 compliance
9.5.2 Design Compliance & 9.5.3 Third-Party
  • Review IFC drawings for alignment with the agreed strategy
  • Issue Stage 4 Waterproofing Sign-Off (design compliance only)
  • Support Building Control and Warranty Provider technical reviews
  • Participate in interviews / technical evaluation of waterproofing subcontractors
Output: Stage 4 Waterproofing Sign-Off (design compliance only)
Stage 5 — Construction Phase Support
Provide proportionate advisory support during construction
9.6.1 Site Attendance
  • Attendance at key milestone inspections (as agreed)
  • Preparation of photographic records and visit reports
9.6.2 Advisory Role
  • Provide high-level, non-supervisory comments
  • Review contractor proposals for sequencing, protection and penetrations
9.6.3 Completion Activities
  • Final site visit (where applicable)
  • Issue Post-Installation Advisory Note
  • Review Operation & Maintenance (O&M) information
9.6.4 CMT (if appointed)
  • Monthly enhanced construction monitoring
  • Issue Release Note confirming no further inspections required
Outputs: Visit reports · Post-Installation Advisory Note · CMT Release Note (if applicable)
Stage 6 — In-Use / Post-Completion Review
Provide limited post-completion advisory input where issues arise
Services at this stage are strictly subject to additional instruction and fee agreement.
  • Review of reported defects or performance concerns
  • Advisory input on remedial strategies
  • Support with warranty or insurance technical queries
Stage 7 — Project Completion & Close-Out
External completion and internal QA close-out
9.8.1 Completion of Services (External)
  • Issue final reports, verification notes or advisory outputs (where included in appointment)
  • Close out all outstanding technical queries relating to agreed scope
  • Confirm completion of services in writing to the client
  • Issue final invoice in accordance with agreed fee terms
9.8.2 Internal Project Close-Out (QA & Risk Management)
  • Update project status to “Complete” in all internal registers
  • Ensure all final deliverables are saved in the project record per CLW QA procedures
  • Record any issues, disputes, deviations from scope or notable lessons learned
  • Identify and flag any PI-sensitive matters, unresolved risks or future exposure
  • Close project file in line with CLW Quality Assurance requirements
9.8.3 Feedback & Continuous Improvement
  • Request client feedback within 2–4 weeks of completion
  • Focus: value added · clarity of advice · willingness to work together again
  • Log feedback (positive or negative) · Share positive feedback internally
  • Use feedback to refine SOPs and proposals
  • Add project to portfolio where appropriate
Responsibility: Practice Director / Project Lead
